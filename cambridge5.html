Cambridge 5 Spy Ring

The Cambridge 5 Spy Ring was a ring of spies in the United Kingdom that passed information to the Soviet Union during World War II and was active from the 1930s until at least into the early 1950s. The project involves the diaries of Guy Liddell, MI5's director of counterespionage at the time.

The goal of the project is to conduct Social Network Analysis on the diary text to create a new representation of the connections between entities related to the spy ring. Social Network Analysis is the investigation of social structure through the use of networks and graph theory.
