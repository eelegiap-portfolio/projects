Word Formation

Word Formation is a tool currently in development for the Visualizing Russian website, a suite of web-based tools for exploring the data behind the Russian language.

This tool explores Словообразование, word formation, which in this case refers to the prefix-root-suffix make-up of different words. The breakdown of words in this way is a common pedagogical tool in Russian language classes, as the word parts carry semantic meaning and aid in word recognition.

Word Formation prominently features a novel D3.js visualization (designed by me) which visualizes words as nodes suspended at a specified distance from the center (the word root). The length of the link is inversely correlated to how frequent or common a word is. Thus, one can find common words (often easier or more familiar to beginners) closer to the center and less common words farther away.

Hovering over nodes gives additional grammatical or semantic information on the word. There are several ways to customize your view, by node coloring or node limiting functions.

This project has been self-led. With support from the other members of the Visualizing Russian team, I am fully responsible for the design and implementation of every aspect of the tool.
