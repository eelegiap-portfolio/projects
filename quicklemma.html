Quick Lemma

Quick Lemma is a tool currently in development for the Visualizing Russian website, a suite of web-based tools for exploring the data behind the Russian language.

This tool explores the different frequency with which Russian words occur, over time, and where. Data is pulled from two differnet sources: the database compiled by Professor Steven Clancy of the Harvard Slavic Department, and the full version of the Russian National Corpus.

The main advantages of this tool is its functionality to compare the data of different lemmas. In the example to the right, I compare the frequencies of the words "president," "leader," and "secretary." The tool outputs the Google N-Gram viewer of the input words, showing the decline of the use of "secretary" and the ascendancy of "president" from the late 20th to the early 21st century.

Besides a graph, there are static representations of frequency and rank for the three words, showing that "president" has the highest frequency, and "leader" has the lowest, possibly do its status as a borrowed word in Russian.

This project has been self-led. With support from the other members of the Visualizing Russian team, I am fully responsible for the design and implementation of every aspect of the tool.
