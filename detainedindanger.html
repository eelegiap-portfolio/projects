Detained in Danger

Detained in Danger is a collaborative project between the Harvard Data Analytics Group and Human Rights First to create a data-based journalism effort to expose the severity of the COVID-19 epidemic within ICE detention facilities.

The self-updating website tracks the number of COVID-19 infections within specific ICE facilities by scraping the official data from the ICE website on a weekly basis. The data takes form of a map of the United States, individual data info-cards for each facility, and a list of detention centers ranked from most to least cases. The interactive data visualizations are supplemented by four investigative articles on the conditions and reality of life inside the facilities.

My work on the project ranged from data collection, the set-up of the JavaScript backend, implementation of the D3.js map, integration of the info-cards, to design aspects of the front page (HTML/CSS). The project was entirely student-created, and I worked with two others to set up the webpage.

Website at https://detainedindanger.org/.
