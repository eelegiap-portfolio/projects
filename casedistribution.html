Case Distribution

Case Distribution is a tool currently in development for the Visualizing Russian website, a suite of web-based tools for exploring the data behind the Russian language.

The tool uses grammatical data scraped from the abridged version of the Russian National Corpus to create an interactive platform for visualizing grammar. In particular, Case Distribution aims to represent the case breakdown of different Russian lemmas. Case breakdowns provide a window into the usage of different words.

The six Russian cases - nominative, accusative, genitive, locative, dative, and instrumental - each occupy their own corner on the radar chart. The lemma is further broken down into its singular and plural forms. The number of each form that occurs in a particular in the abridged corpus is represented by a vertex on the radar chart.

Enter a lemma (dictionary form of a word) and hover over the radar chart to learn more about the word's relative frequency across cases.

This project has been self-led. With support from the other members of the Visualizing Russian team, I am fully responsible for the design and implementation of every aspect of the tool, including data collection.
